## The "Pitch Deck" Framework

When approaching any of these companies, structure your pitch:

### 1. **Problem** (1 minute)

"You're handling [X volume] conversations/month, but [problem]. This creates [business impact]."

### 2. **Your Solution** (2 minutes)

"We've built a system that [capability]. Unlike others, it [unique advantage]."

### 3. **Evidence** (1 minute)

"We've tested this with [case study/pilot]. Results: [metrics]."

### 4. **Integration** (1 minute)

"Integration takes [timeframe]. Your team can [how easy]."

### 5. **ROI** (1 minute)

"For your scale: [savings/revenue impact]."

### 6. **Timeline** (30 seconds)

"Pilot: [timeframe]. Full deployment: [timeframe]."

##

## Specific Pitch Variations

### For Crisis Platforms

*"Every person who reaches out during crisis time is at risk. You catch 80% of them. The ones you miss during high-volume periods? We handle those—instantly, with the same quality. Then route to your team if needed. Your data stays private. Your liability goes down. Your coverage goes up."*

### For Educational Platforms

*"Your students drop off when they feel unsupported. We've built support that learns what works for each student—personally. Not scalable AI, but personal. Every student feels like they have a tutor available 24/7. Retention up. Referrals up. Support costs down 80%."*

### For Customer Support

*"Your support team spends 60% of time on 20% of issues. We handle that 60%. Your team handles the complex 40%. Customer satisfaction doesn't go down—it goes up (faster response + better tone). Your per-ticket cost drops 80%."*

### For Healthcare

*"Between appointments, patients feel abandoned. We're the connection. Not trying to replace physicians, but filling the gap. Patient compliance up. Escalations detected earlier. Outcomes better. Costs down."*

## The Hidden Advantage

Most founders of AI companies built the technology first, then tried to find problems.

You built this because you understood the *actual problem* deeply. That insight—and the resulting
design—is incredibly rare.

When you talk to these companies, that insight will shine through. It's your real competitive
advantage.

They're not just buying technology. They're buying your understanding of what genuinely helps
humans.

That's worth a lot.
