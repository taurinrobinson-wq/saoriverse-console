### The Ophina Backstory (Foundational)

**Who Was Ophina?**
- Age: 5 years old
- Parents: Ravi & Nima (life partners)
- Personality: Precocious, light-seeking, enthralled by marketplace sights/smells/sounds
- Pre-Collapse Moment: Joyful scene — Ravi & Nima watched Ophina play around market stalls, delighted

**The Collapse Incident**
- **What Happened**: Ophina wandered too far into ruins; a civic center wall suddenly collapsed
- **Timeline**: She was chasing a scent or following a curiosity when rubble fell
- **Injury**: Leg trapped under rubble; parents found her alive but unable to escape
- **Sustainment**: Ravi & Nima brought food and water; kept her alive for a period
- **Death**: Eventually succumbed to her injuries while parents watched helplessly
- **Result**: Permanent wound for both Ravi & Nima; they chose to stay in marketplace as "penance" despite unable to feel joy

**Kaelen's Connection**
- Kaelen was present during collapse (stealing from someone's bag)
- Too immersed in theft to intervene
- Holds guilt about Ophina's death
- Provides alternate perspective on collapse to player (if asked)

**Why This Matters**
- Ophina is **not a save-able side quest**; she's permanently dead
- Her death is **the core wound** that drives Ravi & Nima's entire story arc
- The game does NOT allow "fixing" the past; only understanding & integrating loss
- Ophina becomes a **bridge to Velinor**: Velinor has memory of playing with Ophina; this connection suggests Velinor is consciousness, not mere algorithm

### The Five Acts (Complete Story Structure)

#### **ACT 1: MEETING & DISCOVERY** (4 passages)
**Goal**: Establish Ravi/Nima as core NPCs; introduce grief; trigger player emotional engagement

**Passage 1.1: Marketplace Intro**
- Context: Player enters marketplace phase 2 beginning
- NPC: Ravi (agitated, desperate)
- Ravi's Hook (varies by player TONE):
  - High Empathy: *"You have the look of someone who listens. We need someone who listens."*
  - High Skepticism: *"What are you looking at? Unless you can help, keep moving."*
  - Balanced: *"Wait. Do you have a moment?"*
- **Glyph Moment**: Soft blue shimmer (first Sorrow glyph hint; not named yet)
- **Choice 1.1a** (Engage): +15 Empathy, Ravi influence +0.3 → Passage 1.2
- **Choice 1.1b** (Dismiss): +10 Skepticism, Ravi influence locked at low → Passage 1.3 (arc paused)

**Passage 1.2: Ravi's Burden** (If engaged)
- Context: Ravi pulls player aside
- Ravi reveals:
  - *"My partner, Nima... she lost someone. Someone she loved more than anything. Ophina. I..." [pause] "I should have stopped what was coming. I should have been there."*
- **Glyph Moment**: Sorrow glyph becomes visible in Ravi's trembling hand
- **Player State**: If Empathy > 60, player feels Ravi's guilt (coherence +5)
- **Passage Ends**: Nima appears from marketplace crowd

**Passage 1.3: Nima Arrives**
- Context: Nima (smaller, controlled, intense) reaches Ravi
- Nima's Test (varies by Empathy):
  - High Empathy: *"Why are you telling strangers? We handle this ourselves."*
  - Medium: *"Who is this? Do they actually want to know?"*
  - High Skepticism: *"Another con artist?"*
- Player can apologize, ask directly, or maintain distance
- **Choice 1.3a** (Engage): +12 Empathy, +8 Integration, Nima influence +0.4
- **Choice 1.3b** (Distance): +5 Awareness (respects boundaries), arc pauses

**Passage 1.4: Ophina's Memory** (If high engagement)
- Context: Nima sits, Ravi silent beside her, player listens
- Nima begins: *"Ophina was... she was the third of us. Not just partner—she was light. She found meaning in things nobody else could see. She saw beauty in broken things. And then, three months ago, she just... didn't come home."*
- **Glyph Moment**: Remembrance glyph appears (tier 1)
- **Coherence Check**: If < 40, player must make emotional choice (too overwhelmed)
- **Choice 1.4a** (Offer support): +18 Empathy, Nima influence +0.5 → full story
- **Choice 1.4b** (Doubt them): +12 Skepticism, Nima influence +0.2, Ravi -0.1 → fragmented story
- **Choice 1.4c** (Admit confusion): +10 Awareness, both influence +0.3 → leads to invitation

**Passage 1.5: The Invitation** (Commitment Point)
- Context: Ravi and Nima exchange a look; decide something has shifted
- Ravi: *"We're going back to that chamber. We need to know what happened to Ophina. And we need someone with us—someone who can see what we can't. Will you come?"*
- **All three glyphs shimmer together** (Sorrow + Remembrance + Legacy hint — first connection)
- **Choice 1.5a** (Commit): +15 Integration, Ravi & Nima influence +0.5 → ACT 2
- **Choice 1.5b** (Decline): Arc pauses; can resume later (loses influence but not permanently)

### Writing Guidance for Encounters

**When Creating New Encounters**:

1. **Identify the inner thought** (player's emotional reaction)
   - Keep it simple: 5-10 words max
   - Make it relatable: fear, confusion, curiosity, wonder

2. **Design 4 choices** that feel distinct
   - Choice 1: Bold/extroverted action
   - Choice 2: Cautious/introverted restraint
   - Choice 3: Curious/empathetic exploration
   - Choice 4: Passive/observant presence

3. **Assign TONE impacts** (hidden from player)
   - Each choice affects 1-4 TONE stats
   - Keep impacts modest: typically +0.1 to +0.3
   - Make them feel natural to the choice

4. **Determine NPC resonance shifts**
   - Which NPC cares about this choice?
   - Does this choice align with or challenge their values?
   - Update their resonance accordingly (+0.05 to +0.25)

5. **Set the next scene**
   - Each choice should lead somewhere meaningfully different
   - Avoid all choices leading to the same dialogue

---

### Encounter Variations Across the Game

**Pattern 1: Initial Meeting** (What the user just encountered)
- Inner thought focuses on observation: "They're staring at me..."
- Choices determine first impression
- Sets NPC baseline resonance

**Pattern 2: Emotional Moment** (Deepening encounters)
- Inner thought focuses on emotion: "She seems so sad... what should I..."
- Choices determine empathy response
- Shifts NPC trajectory from guarded → vulnerable

**Pattern 3: Moral Crisis** (Choice points with consequences)
- Inner thought focuses on conflict: "I could help... but..."
- Choices determine player values
- Creates branching consequences (alliance, distance, or transformation)

**Pattern 4: Ambiguous Mystery** (Optional deepening)
- Inner thought focuses on confusion: "I don't understand what's happening..."
- Choices determine curiosity vs. caution
- Access to hidden knowledge gated by player approach
