When you divide 1 by the imaginary unit i, you get:


\frac{1}{i} = -i

• Multiply numerator and denominator by the complex conjugate of the denominator (which is –i) to rationalize: $$ \frac{1}{i} = \frac{1 \cdot (-i)}{i \cdot (-i)} = \frac{-i}{-i^2} $$
• Since {F47AC10B-LaTeX-opener} i^2 = -1 {1E8B7F4D-LaTeX-closer}, this becomes: $$ \frac{-i}{-(-1)} = \frac{-i}{1} = -i $$
