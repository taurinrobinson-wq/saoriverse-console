## üß© Passage 1: The False Path

**Narrative Beat:**

## üß© Passage 2: The Fog Logic Puzzle

**Scene:**  
A clearing with five stone markers.  
Each bears a different carved sign ‚Äî emotions made into shapes.

The swamp whispers.  
Not words.  
Just impressions.

**The Five Markers (what they represent, unknown to NPCs):**
1. **Grief** ‚Äî Deep, hollow, pulling downward
2. **Suspicion** ‚Äî Twisted, angular, unsettling
3. **Joy** ‚Äî Curved, reaching upward, almost alive
4. **Anger** ‚Äî Sharp, violent, chaotic marks
5. **Stillness** ‚Äî Quiet, balanced, accepting

But the characters don't know that.  
They only **feel** it.

**Clues:**

- **A whisper in the fog** (heard through high Empathy):  
  > "The one who hides does not run."
  
- **Kaelen** (if present, high Trust):  
  > "Drossel‚Ä¶ he was the quiet sort. Sat more than he walked. Waited instead of rushed."

- **Environmental hint** (high Observation):  
  The stones settle deeper into the earth in order, from agitated to still. The fifth stone has no moss ‚Äî it's too perfectly balanced.

**Player Choice:**

- **Touch the stone that feels quiet, steady, unmoving** (Stillness marker)
  ‚Üí Puzzle succeeds
  ‚Üí Fog clears slightly
  ‚Üí Passage 2 resolves

- **Touch any other stone**
  ‚Üí Puzzle fails
  ‚Üí Fog loops back to Passage 1
  ‚Üí Must re-enter and try again

**Mechanical Detail:**
- **High Observation + High Empathy**: Player intuits the answer immediately
- **High Observation, Low Empathy**: Player solves through logical elimination
- **High Empathy, Low Observation**: Player solves through emotional resonance alone
- **Low both**: Puzzle requires trial-and-error or Kaelen's hint

**Lore Language:**

No one says "glyph."  
They say **"the quiet stone"** or **"the one that feels like waiting."**

> "Drossel always liked the quiet places. Not because he was shy. Because they let him think. And thinking is what made him dangerous."

## ‚úîÔ∏è Why This Version Works

### Lore Consistency
- **No character breaks lore** by referencing lost technology, glyphs, Corelink, or OS fragments
- NPCs don't know what glyphs *are*; they only know what they *feel* like

### Mechanical Transparency
- **The emotional OS still functions** ‚Äî Observation, Empathy, Trust all gate on different puzzle paths
- **Only the player and the engine know the technical truth**
- NPCs experience the world through **intuition, superstition, and emotion**

### Narrative Tone
- Descriptions use **folklore language**: marks, tokens, keepsakes, whispers, omens
- Environmental cues suggest **ancient mystery without naming it**
- NPCs describe phenomena **through what they've witnessed**, not what they understand
- The world feels **haunted, half-remembered, impossibly old**

### Emotional Resonance
- Passage 1 tests **awareness and empathic memory**
- Passage 2 tests **emotional understanding without naming it**
- Passage 3 rewards **emotional growth** with physical tokens and a sense of having witnessed something sacred
