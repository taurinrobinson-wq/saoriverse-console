# Velinor: Dual Canon Reference Guide

## Executive Summary

**Velinor: Remnants of the Tone** exists as two parallel canonical continuities:

1. **Game Canon** (primary continuity): Player receives Codex from Saori
2. **Graphic Novel Canon** (parallel continuity): Lior discovers Codex herself in Archive ruins

**Both are equally valid. Neither contradicts the other. They are intentionally different to serve the narrative needs of each medium.**

---

## The Core Divergence: Codex Origin

### What Changes

| Element | Game Canon | GN Canon |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Protagonist** | Player (unnamed) | Lioren ("Lior") |
| **Codex Giver** | Saori (direct handoff) | Lior (self-discovery) |
| **Context** | Desert encounter | Lower Archive annex |
| **Narrative Function** | Destiny/chosenness | Resonance/attunement |
| **Saori's Role** | Present, active | Absent, mythic |
| **Player/Lior Relationship to Codex** | Given as responsibility | Claimed as personal truth |

### Why These Changes Matter

**Game Canon Benefits:**
- Saori as mentor creates narrative authority
- Direct handoff grounds mechanical systems
- Player receives context and clarity
- Sets up endgame reveal of Corelink system

**GN Canon Benefits:**
- Mystery drives narrative tension
- Lior's independent choice creates character agency
- Saori's absence creates dramatic payoff when she appears
- Codex becomes a catalyst rather than a directive

## Character Manifestations Across Canons

### The Player (Game Canon Only)

**Arrival:** Via Saori encounter (desert context)  
**Codex Relationship:** Given by Saori with context and purpose  
**Role in Story:** Receiver of knowledge; integrator of truths  
**Ending:** Player chooses Corelink fate (Restart vs. Abandon)

---

### Lioren / Lior (GN Canon Only)

**Arrival:** Rural origin; came seeking to understand parents' loss  
**Codex Relationship:** Discovered in Archive ruins; kept secret  
**Role in Story:** Catalyst for Malrik and Elenya's re-remembrance  
**Story Arc:** Volumes 1-2 reveal her as bridge between institutional and spiritual knowledge

---

### Saori

**Game Canon:**
- Active, present protagonist mentor
- Gives Codex directly
- Explains glyph system origin
- Central to mid-game narrative

**GN Canon:**
- Absent from Volume 1
- Referenced through legend, glyphs, rituals
- Appears later (future volumes)
- Her appearance becomes a major revelation moment

---

### Malrik

**Game Canon:**
- Encountered through glyph system
- NPC with specific story arc
- Romantic subplot with Elenya develops across games
- Relationship to player mediated by glyphs

**GN Canon:**
- Volume 1 main character (POV)
- Employer and mentor to Lior
- Unaware of his own hidden love story (until Volume 2)
- Dismisses Codex initially; later regrets this

---

### Elenya

**Game Canon:**
- Encountered through ritual spaces and glyphs
- High Seer with independent spiritual practice
- Romantic subplot with Malrik develops across game

**GN Canon:**
- Appears in Volume 1, but secondary to Malrik
- Recognizes Lior as carrier of sacred object
- Senses the Codex without seeing it
- Her own story (and her love for Malrik) revealed in Volume 2

## Canon Integrity Rules

### Rule 1: Choose Your Continuity

**Before writing any narrative content, explicitly state which continuity it belongs to:**

- ✓ "*In game canon...*"
- ✓ "*In the graphic novel...*"
- ✓ "*In both continuities...*"

### Rule 2: Preserve Each Canon's Version

**Within its own continuity, each canon is absolute and self-coherent:**

- Game: Saori gives Codex. This is not negotiable in game contexts.
- GN: Lior finds Codex. This is not negotiable in GN contexts.

### Rule 3: No Cross-Medium Mixing

**Do not:**
- Suggest game player discovers Codex (that's GN-specific)
- Suggest Lior receives Codex from Saori in early volumes (that's game-specific)
- Mix narrative beats across continuities

**Do:**
- Treat each continuity as complete and valid
- Document convergence points explicitly
- Allow later volumes/game sections to reference the parallel canon

### Rule 4: Convergence is Revelation, Not Retcon

**Convergence moments (where canons reference each other) should feel like:**
- Discovery: "Oh, there was always another path!"
- Integration: "Both truths lead to the same place!"
- Expansion: "The story is larger than either continuity alone!"

**NOT like:**
- Contradiction: "One was wrong the whole time"
- Retcon: "We're changing what happened"
- Error: "We made a mistake and need to fix it"

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Why have two canons if they cover the same story?

**A:** Because different mediums require different narrative structures:
- **Game:** Needs mechanical clarity, player agency, direct mentorship
- **Graphic Novel:** Needs visual mystery, character discovery, independent awakening

Both tell the same *themes* but through different *forms*.

### Q2: Which canon is "more correct"?

**A:** Both are equally correct within their continuity. There is no hierarchy.

### Q3: Will the canons ever merge?

**A:** Possibly, but only through explicit convergence moments that enrich both:
- Example: Player encounters a vision/text referencing Lior's discovery
- Example: Lior learns that Saori placed the Codex for her to find
- Example: Both paths lead to the same endgame, revealing why divergence was necessary

### Q4: What if I'm writing about Malrik and Elenya—which canon do I use?

**A:** Their love story is **shared canon** (pre-collapse origin, collapse trauma, post-collapse amnesia). But their interactions with the player/Lior are canon-specific:
- Game: Player mediates their re-remembrance
- GN: Lior (through Codex) catalyzes their re-remembrance

### Q5: Can a character appear in both canons?

**A:** Yes. NPCs can exist in both. But their perspective on events differs:
- Malrik in game: meets Player through glyph system
- Malrik in GN: meets Lior through Archive employment
- Malrik's core character remains consistent; context changes

### Q6: What about Corelink choice—does it matter in both canons?

**A:** Yes, but differently:
- Game: Player's choice (Restart vs. Abandon) determines ending
- GN: Lior's journey may eventually lead to a similar choice, but her path is different

## Version Control & Updates

### How to Document Changes

When updating dual-canon material:

1. **State which continuity is affected**
2. **Preserve the other continuity exactly**
3. **Note any new convergence points**
4. **Update this reference guide**

### Example Commit Message

```
Add Malrik GN Volume 1 character notes

- Preserve game canon where Saori gives Codex
- Add GN canon where Malrik dismisses Codex
- Document tension between Malrik's institutional philosophy and Elenya's spiritual approach
- Note foreshadowing of regret (dismissal becomes significant later)
- Both continuities remain independent and valid
```
