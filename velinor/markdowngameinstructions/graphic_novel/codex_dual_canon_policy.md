# Codex Origin — Dual Canon Documentation

## Overview

**Velinor: Remnants of the Tone** exists across two parallel media with intentionally different narrative structures:

1. **Game Canon** (primary continuity)
2. **Graphic Novel Canon** (parallel continuity)

Both are canonical within their respective mediums. This document formally establishes how the Codex origin differs and why these differences serve narrative function, not lore inconsistency.

---

## Game Canon: Saori as the Giver

### Origin

- The Codex is given **directly to the player by Saori**.
- This occurs during a **desert encounter** in the game's early acts.
- The player has no prior connection to Saori; this is their first meeting.

### Narrative Function

- **Establishes Saori as architect**: Saori is revealed as co-creator of the glyph system and the Codex itself.
- **Ties Codex to endgame**: The handoff from Saori creates narrative through-line to the final chamber reveal.
- **Chosenness through destiny**: The player is selected by Saori for a reason; the Codex is not accident but design.
- **Authority transfer**: Saori passes her knowledge and responsibility to the player explicitly.

### Game Mechanical Function

- Codex unlocks the **glyph system** for gameplay.
- Serves as **journal** for narrative tracking.
- Acts as **dialogue device** between player and NPC glyphs.
- Enables **player agency** (choosing which glyphs to pursue, in what order).

### Dialogue Example (Game Canon)

> **Saori:** "You are not lost. You are unremembered. This will help you listen."  
> *(She places the Codex in your hands. It is warm.)*  
> **Saori:** "When you hold it, you will see what the collapse fragmented. The glyphs are memory made visible. Use them to understand."

---

## Graphic Novel Canon: Lior as the Discoverer

### Origin

- The Codex is **discovered by Lioren ("Lior")** while working in the **lower Archive annex**.
- She finds it partially buried in dust and debris during her first week of employment under Malrik.
- The Codex activates **only when Lior touches it**, revealing a faint, incomplete glyph.
- Malrik initially dismisses it as a "low-priority pre-collapse artifact."

### Narrative Function

- **Grounds story in Lior's POV**: The discovery is her first moment of unusual connection; it is *her* story, not handed to her.
- **Keeps Saori off-screen and mythic**: Saori's absence in Volume 1 makes her appearance later more impactful.
- **Codex as catalyst, not bestowed gift**: The object chooses Lior through resonance, not through Saori's deliberate transfer.
- **Creates mystery**: Lior doesn't know where the Codex came from, who created it, or why it responds to her.
- **Establishes agency**: Lior keeps the Codex secret from Malrik, making an independent decision that defines her character.

### Graphic Novel Mechanical Function

- Codex triggers **Lior's emotional awakening** to the glyph system.
- Serves as **visual device** (the glyph manifestations are drawn, not UI-based).
- Creates **tension** (Malrik doesn't understand it; Elenya senses something but is cautious).
- Enables **Lior's independence** (she is not chosen by authority, but by resonance).

### Dialogue Example (GN Canon)

> **Malrik:** "Tag it, shelve it, and move on. We don't have time for curiosities. Artifacts don't choose people, Lior. They survive or they don't."  
> *(He walks away. Lior holds the object, turns it over.)*  
> **Internal Monologue (Lior):** "But this one just did."

## Reconciliation: How Both Exist Simultaneously

### The Key Distinction

**The player in the game is not Lior.**  
**Lior is the protagonist of the graphic novel.**

These are **different characters in different mediums**, not the same character with contradictory origins.

- **Game Player**: Receives Codex from Saori. Saori's role is central to game narrative.
- **Lior (GN Protagonist)**: Discovers Codex herself. Saori is absent from early volumes.

### Why This Works

1. **Narrative Design for Each Medium**

   - **Game**: Benefits from direct mentorship and clear narrative structure. Saori's authority gives the player mechanical/narrative grounding.
   - **Graphic Novel**: Benefits from mystery and discovery. Lior's independent awakening creates visual and emotional narrative texture.

2. **Thematic Resonance**

   - **Game**: Theme is *integration*—learning to hold multiple perspectives, multiple truths. Saori guides this process.
   - **Graphic Novel**: Theme is *discovery*—learning to trust resonance over authority, feeling over institution. Lior guides this process.

3. **Canon Integrity**

   - Neither continuity contradicts the other if they are understood as **parallel, intentional variations** rather than retcons.
   - Both versions of the Codex origin are **true** within their respective continuities.

### Timeline Compatibility

**Game Canon:**
- Player arrives at marketplace via Saori encounter (early game).
- Player receives Codex directly.
- Player begins glyph-gathering immediately.

**Graphic Novel Canon:**
- Lior has been in marketplace for at least one week.
- Lior discovers Codex while working for Malrik.
- Lior begins quiet exploration of glyphs independently.

**Possible Convergence Point (future):**
If the graphic novel eventually reaches the endgame events, Lior might encounter Saori in later volumes. This encounter would then reveal *why* Saori ensured the Codex was present in the Archive for Lior to find—suggesting Saori's hand was present even in "discovery," but hidden from Lior (and the reader) until that moment.

## Canon Policy for All Future Narrative Work

### 1. Preserve Integrity Within Each Medium

**In Game Contexts:**
- Saori gives the Codex to the player.
- This is the canonical game origin; it is not negotiable or alternative.
- All game narrative work presumes this handoff.

**In Graphic Novel Contexts:**
- Lior discovers the Codex herself in the Archive ruins.
- This is the canonical GN origin; it is not negotiable or alternative.
- All GN narrative work presumes this discovery.

### 2. Avoid Cross-Medium Mixing

- **Never** suggest the game player discovers the Codex themselves (that is GN canon).
- **Never** suggest Lior receives the Codex from Saori in early GN volumes (that is game canon).
- Treat each canon as internally coherent and complete.

### 3. Document Divergence Explicitly

For any narrative element that differs between game and GN:

- Create a **"Dual Canon" documentation file** (like this one).
- Specify which continuity each version belongs to.
- Explain the narrative function of the difference.
- Lock in the version for each medium.

### 4. Allow Convergence Gracefully

Later in the story (game endgame, GN later volumes), the two continuities *may* reference each other or reveal unexpected connections:

- Example: Saori reveals she placed the Codex in the Archive for Lior to discover.
- Example: The player encounters a text or vision referencing Lior's discovery.
- Example: A character recognizes parallels between the two paths.

**But this convergence is not retcon.** It is thematic reconciliation: both origins were true; both paths lead to the same glyph system; both protagonists serve the larger story.

## Character Notes for Each Canon

### Malrik

**Game Canon:**
- Met by the player through the glyph system.
- Becomes accessible once glyphs are discovered.
- His relationship to the player is mediated by the Codex and glyphs.

**Graphic Novel Canon (Volume 1):**
- Lior's employer.
- Dismisses the Codex as a low-priority artifact.
- His dismissal becomes a point of tension/growth later (he realizes he misunderstood something sacred).
- His philosophy: institutions preserve knowledge; individual resonance is secondary.

---

### Elenya

**Game Canon:**
- Met by the player through ritual encounters or the glyph system.
- Represents spiritual alternative to Malrik's institutional approach.

**Graphic Novel Canon (Volume 1):**
- Does not witness Codex discovery.
- Later senses that Lior carries something resonant (Volume 2 transition).
- Interprets the Codex through spiritual language before it is formally named.
- Her awareness hints that Lior's path is more sacred than institutional.

---

### Saori

**Game Canon:**
- Active, present in desert encounter.
- Gives Codex directly.
- Explains the glyph system's origin and purpose (from player's perspective).
- Central to game's mythic arc.

**Graphic Novel Canon (Volume 1):**
- Absent from direct narrative.
- Possibly referenced in legends, glyphs, or Elenya's spiritual teachings.
- Her presence is felt through the Codex itself (which is revealed as her creation, later).
- Entry into the narrative is delayed, making her eventual appearance impactful.

---

### Velinor

**Game Canon:**
- Present in final chamber.
- Co-architect of Corelink system alongside Saori.
- Encountered at narrative climax.

**Graphic Novel Canon:**
- Not appearing in Volume 1.
- May appear in later volumes as story reaches similar climax.
- Relationship to Lior is different from relationship to game player (because their paths diverge earlier).

---

### Lioren / Lior (GN Protagonist)

**Not applicable to game canon.**

Lior exists only in graphic novel continuity. She is not the game player.

**Game Player (Unnamed):**
- Not applicable to graphic novel canon.
- The player exists only in game continuity.

This separation is intentional and preserves both narratives' integrity.

---

## Summary: The Principle

**The Codex has two canonical origins because the two mediums serve different narrative functions:**

- **Game**: Direct mentorship, mechanical clarity, destiny-through-authority.
- **Graphic Novel**: Independent discovery, visual mystery, resonance-over-institution.

Both are true. Neither invalidates the other.

All future narrative work must:
1. Choose which continuity a work belongs to.
2. Implement that continuity's version consistently.
3. Document any convergence points explicitly.
4. Treat the other continuity as parallel truth, not error.

This policy ensures that Velinor's world can expand across multiple mediums without lore collapse or retcon confusion.
