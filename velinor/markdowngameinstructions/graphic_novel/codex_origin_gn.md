# Codex Origin — Graphic Novel Canon (Lioren)

## Context

This origin applies **only** to the graphic novel continuity  
(e.g., *Velinor: The Archivist and the Seer, Vol. 1* and subsequent volumes).

In this continuity, Lioren ("Lior") **discovers** the Codex herself while working in the marketplace ruins.  
Saori does **not** appear in person in Volume 1.

---

## Scene: First Discovery (Working Title: "Dust and Echoes")

**Location:** Lower Archive Annex / partially collapsed records wing beneath the main Archive building.  
**Time:** Early in Lior's employment with Malrik, within her first week in the marketplace.  
**Participants:** Lior, Malrik (briefly), background archivists (non-essential).

---

### Beat 1 — Assigned to the Dust

Malrik assigns Lior to a low-status task:

- She is told to help clear debris and sort "non-critical artifacts" in a side chamber.
- Malrik's attitude: curt, skeptical, treating her as untrained labor.
- The work is isolated; she is alone for most of this sequence.
- The chamber is dim, musty, filled with broken shelves and scattered pages.

**Dialogue (Malrik):**
> "Lior. There's a collapsed section on the lower level. Debris needs sorting. Don't break anything expensive. Tag what survives, shelve what matters. Leave the rest."

Lior's internal response:
- She is used to dismissal from authority figures.
- She doesn't complain; she simply begins the work.

---

### Beat 2 — The Object That Doesn't Fit

While clearing a collapsed shelf:

- Lior uncovers a **compact, palm-sized device** partially embedded in dust and stone.
- It is not shaped like a book, tablet, or known Archive tool.
- Visual description:
  - Smooth surfaces, worn but not damaged
  - Faint etched lines running in concentric patterns
  - No obvious opening or mechanism
  - Color: somewhere between bronze and silver, darkened with age
  - Weight: heavier than it appears, as if made of denser material than stone

Lior's physical reaction:

- She brushes dust away carefully, almost reverently.
- As her fingers touch the surface, she feels a subtle **vibration**—not mechanical, but organic, like a held breath.
- Her hands stall mid-movement.

Internal monologue:
> "This isn't supposed to be down here. This is... careful. Placed, not lost."

---

### Beat 3 — Malrik's Dismissal

Lior brings the object up to show Malrik:

- She wipes it clean and carries it carefully in both hands.
- She finds him cataloguing near the Archive's central desk.
- She approaches quietly, respectfully—expecting dismissal, resigned to it.

Malrik glances at the object:

- His initial reaction is neutral, almost bored.
- He picks it up, turns it over, sets it down.
- He categorizes it immediately as:
  - "Pre-collapse artifact. Unknown function. Low priority."

His instruction:

> "Tag it, shelve it, and move on. We don't have time for curiosities."

Key line (reflecting his philosophy):

> "Artifacts don't choose people, Lior. They survive or they don't. The rest is sentiment."

He returns to his work, dismissing her with his body language before his voice even stops.

Lior's reaction:

- She nods, accepting.
- But something in her **resists** this conclusion.
- She doesn't say it aloud.

---

### Beat 4 — The First Activation

Left alone again, back in the lower chamber:

- Lior sits on a piece of fallen stonework, turning the object over in her hands.
- She studies the etched lines, trying to find a pattern or meaning.
- Her thumb passes over a particular groove—a spot that felt warm when Malrik touched it, now cooler.
- As she traces it, the surface **flickers**.

Visual effect:

- Not a sudden glow, but a tremor of light.
- Like a reflection catching and un-catching.
- At the center: a **single, incomplete glyph** appears—dim, unstable, like an afterimage.

Glyph description:

- It is not fully formed.
- It resembles a half-completed circle with jagged edges breaking away.
- Colors cycle: amber, silver, deep green, then fade to nothing.
- The glyph lasts only 2-3 seconds before vanishing.

Physical/emotional response in Lior:

The moment the glyph appears, Lior experiences:
- **Vertigo**: the room tilts slightly, not from dizziness but from a sudden sense of *depth*
- **Warmth in her chest**: not painful, but present—like something that was asleep has opened its eyes
- A sense of **being seen** rather than seeing
- A whisper (not audible, but felt): *"Here. You are here."*

Recovery:

- The glyph fades.
- The world stabilizes.
- Lior sits in silence for several moments.
- Her hands are trembling slightly.

Internal realization:

> "It responded. To me. That isn't... that shouldn't..."

She looks at the object again. It is inert, silent, but no longer *empty*.

---

### Beat 5 — Lior's Private Decision

Instead of shelving it as instructed:

- Lior turns the object over in her hands one final time.
- She examines the etched lines in the fading light, memorizing their pattern.
- She makes a deliberate choice.

Decision point:

Option A (recommended):
- She keeps the object close—in a small pouch she wears under her clothes, beneath the Archive uniform.
- It rests against her collarbone, warm against her skin.

Option B (alternative):
- She keeps it in a pocket, but always aware of its weight.
- She touches it throughout the day, feeling for another flicker.

Either way:

- She does **not** tell Malrik what happened.
- She does **not** yet tell Elenya.
- She tags a different artifact and shelves it, completing the assigned work.
- When asked about the small object later, if asked at all, she will lie easily: "Broken mechanism. Nothing salvageable."

Internal monologue (final):

> "This doesn't belong on a shelf with things that are already dead. It belongs with someone who can feel it. Someone who needs to remember. Or learn to."

> "Malrik said artifacts don't choose people. But this one just did."

---

## The Codex Recognized

This scene establishes:

1. **The Codex is not an institution**—it is an *entity* that recognizes emotional resonance.
2. **Lior is specifically attuned**—the object responded to her, not to Malrik, not to Archive systems.
3. **Secrecy is choice**—Lior decides to keep the Codex hidden, not because she's reckless, but because she understands it is *hers* in a way that defies Malrik's institutional logic.
4. **Incomplete knowledge is dangerous**—Neither Lior nor Malrik fully understands what the Codex is. Lior's discovery is the beginning of her *ignorant courage*.

---

## Functional Canon

- The object is the **Codex**, but it is not named as such immediately.
- The term "Codex" is introduced later in the narrative, either:
  - by Malrik after repeated anomalous behavior forces him to reconsider, or
  - by Elenya through spiritual language ("a vessel of memory" or "a listener").
- The **first glyph** is incomplete and unnamed—more sensation than symbol.
  - It represents the Codex in its earliest, most unstable state.
  - Later, as Lior becomes more attuned, glyphs will appear more fully formed.
- This scene is the **graphic novel inciting incident** for Lior's connection to the glyph system.

## Relationship to Game Canon

**This is intentionally different from game canon, not contradictory to it.**

| Aspect | Game Canon | GN Canon |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Codex Giver | Saori (direct handoff) | Lior (self-discovery) |
| Setting | Desert encounter | Archive ruins, lower annex |
| Narrative Function | Destiny / chosenness | Resonance / emotional attunement |
| Knowledge | Given with context | Discovered in confusion |
| Saori's Role | Active, present | Mythic, absent |

Both are canonical within their respective continuities. Neither invalidates the other.

---

## Constraints & Continuity Locks

To preserve this canon while integrating with existing Velinor lore:

1. **Saori does not appear in person in Volume 1 (GN).**
   - Any later Saori references must treat her as mythic, distant, known through glyphs or stories.
   - If Saori appears in later GN volumes, her introduction must reconcile with Lior's independent discovery.

2. **The Codex's pre-history is unclear to Lior.**
   - She does not know who created it or how it ended up in the lower Archive annex.
   - This mystery becomes a thread that connects to the larger narrative (potentially revealing Saori's role much later).

3. **Malrik's dismissal creates tension.**
   - His initial categorization of the Codex as "low-priority" becomes a point of quiet regret later.
   - As the volumes unfold, he realizes he may have dismissed something sacred.

4. **Elenya's awareness comes later.**
   - She does not witness the discovery, but later senses that Lior carries something resonant.
   - Her spiritual framework allows her to perceive what Malrik's institutional logic missed.

---

## Visual Notes for Graphic Novelist

- **The Codex object**: Compact, palm-sized, bronze-silver color, worn but unblemished. Etched with concentric circular patterns.
- **The first glyph**: Incomplete, jagged edges, cycling between amber/silver/deep green. Visible for 2-3 seconds before fading.
- **Lior's expression during activation**: Eyes widen slightly, breath catches, hands steady despite internal turmoil. Moment of vulnerable wonder.
- **Malrik's dismissal**: Professional, tired, not cruel—just institutional. He doesn't know what he's dismissing.
- **The lower Archive annex**: Dim, dusty, architectural decay. Collapsed shelves, broken pages, scattered artifacts. A place forgotten by time.

---

## This Scene's Narrative Weight

"Dust and Echoes" is the **first turning point** of Volume 1:
- Before: Lior is an outsider, tolerated, unmoored, grieving.
- During: She finds something that *recognizes* her.
- After: She is no longer just a worker in the Archive. She is a *carrier* of something she doesn't yet understand.

The Codex doesn't explain itself. It doesn't name itself. It simply *opens* a door in Lior's consciousness. The rest of the volumes will be her—and the reader—learning what's on the other side.
