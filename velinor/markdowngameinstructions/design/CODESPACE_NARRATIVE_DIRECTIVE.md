🜁 **CODESPACE NARRATIVE DIRECTIVE — VELINOR STORYLINE GENERATION**

---

## 1. Emotional Domains Define Every Storyline
Each glyph storyline must be rooted in one or more of Velinor’s emotional domains:

- **Legacy** — ancestry, inheritance, continuity  
- **Ache** — loss, grief, longing  
- **Trust** — community, interdependence, loyalty  
- **Collapse** — fracture, distortion, dissociation  
- **Sovereignty** — boundaries, choice, clarity  
- **Presence** — silence, touch, witness  
- **Joy** — play, reunion, creative spark  

NPCs may bridge multiple domains if their life naturally touches more than one emotional truth.

---

## 2. Memory Loss Is the Core World Mechanic
All NPCs begin with partial or fractured memories. The player’s presence helps them recover:

- identity  
- lineage  
- emotional truth  
- relationships  
- rituals  

Recovery is **non‑mechanical** and happens through:

- witnessing  
- noticing  
- dialogue  
- item collection  
- emotional OS checks  

NPCs never “remember everything” at once — memory returns in fragments.

---

## 3. Glyphs Emerge From Emotional Revelation
A glyph is not a reward or quest item. It is the crystallization of an emotional moment.

Glyphs manifest when:

- an NPC reaches an emotional threshold  
- a memory fragment reconnects  
- a lineage is restored  
- a fear is confronted  
- a choice is made  
- a fracture becomes visible  
- a ritual is completed or witnessed  

Glyphs should feel like **emotional truths made visible**.

---

## 4. Storylines Follow a Three‑Act Emotional Structure
Every NPC arc should follow this pattern:

### Act 1 — Disruption
The NPC shows confusion, fracture, or emotional imbalance. The player notices something is “off.”

### Act 2 — Recognition
The player engages, observes, or collects something that reveals the deeper emotional truth.

### Act 3 — Resolution
A glyph manifests when the emotional truth becomes clear. Resolution may be healing, clarity,
collapse, or acceptance.

Some arcs may have:

- **branching paths** (Kaelen)  
- **multi‑domain arcs** (Sealina)  
- **optional glyphs** (Stolen Memory)  

But all must follow the emotional progression.

---

## 5. Player Interaction Is Observational, Not Mechanical
The player does **not** perform dances, solve puzzles, or mimic actions.

Instead, they:

- watch  
- listen  
- follow  
- notice  
- choose dialogue  
- collect items  
- stay present  

The emotional OS determines what the player perceives or unlocks.

---

## 6. Branching Logic Must Converge
When arcs branch:

- each branch reveals a different emotional truth  
- each branch may give a different glyph  
- **but all branches must converge on the same narrative endpoint**  

Example: Kaelen’s Trust vs Collapse branches both lead to Drossel.

---

## 7. Glyph Summaries Must Be Short and Index‑Friendly
Each glyph entry must include:

- **Domain**  
- **NPC**  
- **Location**  
- **Short Summary (2–4 sentences)**  

The summary should capture the emotional essence, not the full scene.

---

## 8. Use These Two Storylines as Templates

### Sealina — Ache → Legacy Arc
- **Ache (Echoed Longing):**  
Sealina forgets her dance mid‑performance. The player follows her and discovers faded photos of her
mother and grandmother. The glyph manifests when the player witnesses her struggle to reconnect with
the ache of a lineage her body remembers but her mind has lost.

- **Legacy (Hopeful Transmission):**  
The player finds scattered photographs of Sealina’s family. When the images resonate through the
Corelink, the glyph emerges. Returning it restores her memory and allows her to complete her
inherited dance.

### Kaelen — Trust ↔ Collapse Arc
- **Trust (Apprehension):**  
In trickster form, Kaelen sends the player to retrieve a glyph “caught between fear and faith.” If
aligned with him, the player receives the glyph and the true route.

- **Collapse (Stolen Memory):**  
If the player opposes him, Kaelen leads them into the maze. The glyph at the end restores his
fractured identity.

These arcs demonstrate:

- multi‑stage emotional progression  
- memory‑based storytelling  
- optional glyphs  
- branching alignment logic  
- emotional OS gating  
- non‑mechanical player interaction  

Codespace should model all future storylines on these patterns.

### 1. Every storyline must emerge from memory loss.
NPCs begin with fractured identity, missing history, or emotional amnesia. The player’s presence
helps them recover:

- a memory  
- a relationship  
- a ritual

- a boundary  

### 2. The player does not perform complex mechanics.
Interaction is:

- observing  
- listening  
- following  
- noticing  
- collecting  
- choosing dialogue  

### 3. Glyphs manifest at emotional thresholds.
A glyph appears when:

- a truth is revealed  
- a fracture becomes visible  
- a lineage reconnects  
- a fear is confronted  
- a boundary is defined  
- a ritual is completed or witnessed  

### 4. Storylines follow a 3‑act emotional structure.

**Act 1 — Disruption**  
NPC shows confusion, fracture, or emotional imbalance.

**Act 2 — Recognition**  
Player notices the deeper emotional truth.

**Act 3 — Resolution**

## HOW CODESPACE SHOULD USE THIS GUIDE

When generating a new storyline:

1. **Choose a domain.** 2. **Identify the NPC’s emotional fracture.** 3. **Define what memory is
missing.** 4. **Determine what the player must witness, collect, or choose.** 5. **Let the glyph
manifest at the emotional turning point.** 6. **Write a short 2–4 sentence summary for the
organizer.**

This ensures every new storyline:

- fits Velinor’s emotional OS  
- respects the memory‑loss structure  
- avoids mechanical complexity  
- aligns with existing arcs  
- feels like part of the same world

## MAJOR STORYLINE TEMPLATE #3 — PRESENCE DOMAIN
## NPC: Helia, Shrine Healer
## Glyph: Glyph of Quiet Return
## Domain: Presence
## Location: Shrine Healing Alcove (or a new quiet space if you prefer)

---

## FULL STORYLINE (FOR YOUR NARRATIVE DOC)

### Act 1 — Disruption (2)
The player finds Helia tending to a survivor who is unconscious but breathing. Helia looks exhausted
— not physically, but emotionally hollow.

She says:

> “They wake sometimes.  
> But not fully.  
> They reach for someone who isn’t here.”

She admits she doesn’t know who the survivor is. She only knows they were found clutching a broken
Corelink pendant.

Helia has been sitting with them for days, speaking softly, humming, holding their hand — but she
feels like she’s failing.

She says:

> “I don’t know if they can hear me.  
> I don’t know if I’m helping.  
> I don’t know if presence matters when memory is gone.”

This is the fracture:
**Helia doubts the value of presence itself.**

---

### Act 2 — Recognition (2)
The player returns later and finds Helia asleep, head resting on the survivor’s cot.

The survivor is awake — barely — whispering a single word:

> “Stay…”

The player can choose to:

- Sit quietly  
- Hold the survivor’s hand  
- Simply remain in the room  
- Say nothing  

No dialogue is required. No action is required. Just **presence**.

Helia wakes and sees the player sitting with the survivor. She watches silently.

The survivor’s breathing steadies. Their eyes close peacefully.

Helia whispers:

> “Maybe presence is the bridge.  
> Maybe it’s what returns us to ourselves.”

---

### Act 3 — Resolution (2)
As the room settles into shared quiet, the **Glyph of Quiet Return** manifests in the dust near the
cot.

It represents:

- presence as healing  
- silence as connection  
- the return of self through witness  
- the restoration of dignity  

Helia places a hand over the glyph and says:

> “You didn’t fix anything.  
> You didn’t try to.  
> You just stayed.  
> That’s what brought them back.”

The survivor wakes fully the next day.

Helia’s faith in presence is restored.
