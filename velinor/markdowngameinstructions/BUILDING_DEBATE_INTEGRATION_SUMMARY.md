# Building Debate Integration: Glyphs Modified

## Overview

Five key glyphs from the JSON Glyph_Organizer have been modified to weave together:
1. **The archive building debate** (from Narrative Spine) as the central conflict
2. **The love story between Malrik and Elenya** (subtle, subtext-driven)
3. **Coren's role as the paradox-holder** (mediation as true strength)

Rather than creating new glyphs, existing glyphs were recontextualized to become chapters of this integrated narrative arc.

## The Five Modified Glyphs

### **Glyph 24: Glyph of Boundary Stone** (Malrik - Sovereignty Domain)

**From:** Desert Trial Grounds (abstract teaching about boundaries)  
**To:** The Shared Archive Building (Marketplace)

**What Changed:**
- **Setting:** Now explicitly at the newly reclaimed archive building

## The Love Story Woven Through

The love story is **not overt**. It's embedded in:

**Malrik's Glyphs:**
- His fear masking beneath rigidity (Boundary Stone)
- His lingering gaze on her offerings (Measured Step)
- His discipline as a cage for longing

**Elenya's Glyphs:**
- Her hurt at being dismissed (Covenant Flame)
- The way she avoids his eye
- Her persistent effort to show him their visions can coexist

**Coren's Role:**
- She sees what they won't admit to each other
- She holds the space where integration becomes possible
- She teaches that paradox is where love actually lives

**The Player's Role:**
- Witness first, then participant
- The player's choices shape whether Malrik and Elenya can rebuild together
- The player's emotional response to their conflict ripples into the city's future

## Building → Collapse → Reconstruction as Fractal

All three modified Malrik/Elenya glyphs ripple toward the **Building Collapse** turning point:

1. **Boundary Stone** — Establishes what will be lost if the building falls
2. **Measured Step** — Shows the emotional infrastructure that will crumble
3. **Covenant Flame** — Demonstrates what could be preserved if they rebuild together

When the building collapses in Act III:
- If Malrik/Elenya haven't begun to integrate, they blame each other and walk away
- If they've been moving toward Shared Survival, they choose to rebuild together
- The player's choice in Act IV (Triad Convergence) determines which path to the ending

## The Philosophical Through-Line

**Three Scales, One Lesson:**

- **Personal:** Malrik and Elenya's struggle to hold both preservation and activation
- **Communal:** The shared building and whether two factions can inhabit the same space
- **Civilizational:** Velhara's original attempt to manufacture coherence through Corelink instead of earning it through human connection

The glyphs now embody this: **Systems cannot do what people cannot.**

Both Malrik and Elenya must learn to hold paradox. Only then can the building be shared. Only then can Velhara truly be restored.
