## How to Guide Players Toward Specific Endings

### **If you want players to achieve Ending 4 (The Earned Synthesis):**
- Make Archive NPCs dialogue-rich and emotionally rewarding
- Have Coren explicitly note when player is modeling synthesis
- Show Malrik/Elenya's separation after collapse as a turning point where player *must* intervene
- Make player choose "Abandon the Corelink" feel empowering, not terrifying
- Show post-game that people *did* succeed without the system

### **If you want players to naturally find Ending 1 (Hopeful Synthesis):**
- Make system restart feel like the natural choice (most players' instinct)
- Reward Archive/Present Circle bonding early and often
- Show Malrik/Elenya rebuilding together as a natural consequence of player's influence
- The ending "validates" the system as tool rather than master

### **If you want players to discover Ending 3 (Honest Collapse):**
- Make Wound Bearers compelling teachers about system failure
- Show that no amount of bonding can force Malrik/Elenya together if player didn't actively bridge them
- Make "Abandon the Corelink" feel like the only honest choice
- Show post-game that this path takes longest but builds deepest trust

### **If you want players to experience Ending 6 (The Stalemate):**
- Design game so mixed choices are natural and reasonable
- Show Malrik/Elenya partially rebuilding without system
- Make both "Restart" and "Abandon" feel uncertain
- Show that uncertainty itself is authenticâ€”no clear right answer

## The Philosophy Behind All Six

All six endings express the core Fourth Layer truth:

**"Systems cannot do what people cannot."**

- **Endings 1 & 4** explore what happens when people *can* (synthesis) â€” system is secondary
- **Endings 2 & 3** explore what happens when people *can't* (fracture) â€” system is irrelevant  
- **Endings 5 & 6** explore what happens when people *are learning* (partial) â€” system is partner or burden

The player's job isn't to "win" an ending. It's to discover which philosophy they embodied through their choices, and see that philosophy reflected in the world's transformation.
